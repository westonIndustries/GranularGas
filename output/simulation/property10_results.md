# Property 10: Efficiency Impact Monotonicity

**Validates: Requirements 4.2, 3.2**

## Property Statement

Higher efficiency results in lower or equal therms

## Test Results

| End-Use | Low Efficiency | High Efficiency | Improvement Potential |
|---------|---|---|---|
| Space Heating | 0.70 | 0.95 | 26.3% |
| Water Heating | 0.50 | 0.85 | 41.2% |
| Cooking | 0.60 | 0.90 | 33.3% |

## Key Findings

1. **Space Heating**: 26.3% reduction in therms from efficiency improvement
2. **Water Heating**: 41.2% reduction in therms from efficiency improvement
3. **Cooking**: 33.3% reduction in therms from efficiency improvement

## Conclusion

PASS - All end-uses demonstrate the expected inverse relationship between efficiency and consumption. Higher efficiency equipment consistently produces lower therms values, validating the simulation model's physical correctness.
